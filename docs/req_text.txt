Executive SummaryPharmaSys is a comprehensive pharmacy management platform designed for the Indian market, targeting retail standalone pharmacies, hospital / clinic-attached pharmacies, and chain pharmacy networks. The system addresses the complete operational lifecycle of a modern Indian pharmacy — from customer billing and prescription validation to inventory management, purchase order workflows, quotation management, vendor payments, expiry tracking, and regulatory compliance.This document consolidates all business requirements gathered from stakeholder interviews, existing design artefacts, workflow diagrams, and operational notes. It also provides a technical architecture blueprint that development teams can use to design, build, and test the system.KEY OBJECTIVES: • Digitise the end-to-end pharmacy operation for Indian retail & hospital pharmacies • Ensure compliance with India&apos;s Drug & Cosmetics Act, CDSCO regulations, and GST framework • Enable multi-role workflow with granular access control • Support multi-branch (chain) operations with centralised reporting • Provide real-time inventory visibility and automated reorder triggers • Integrate with vendor ecosystems (FarmaRack and similar platforms)2. Business Use Cases5.1 Customer Billing & InvoicingThe billing module handles all customer-facing transactions at the pharmacy counter. It is triggered by a customer arriving with or without a prescription.UC-BIL-001: Full Billing – All Drugs AvailableGIVEN: A customer arrives at the shop with a valid prescription.WHEN: The front desk validates the prescription against available drugs AND finds all drugs available.THEN: The front desk initiates the full billing process. An invoice is generated with all prescribed medicines. GST is applied per medicine HSN/slab. The invoice is printed and payment is collected.UC-BIL-002: Partial Billing – Alternative Drugs AcceptedGIVEN: A customer arrives with a valid prescription. Some drugs are not available in inventory.WHEN: The front desk identifies alternative drugs with the same composition. The customer accepts the alternatives.THEN: The front desk initiates the full billing process including alternatives. Notes the original missing drug for replenishment tracking.NOTE: Alternative drug substitution must be captured in the bill as a line note for audit purposes.UC-BIL-003: Partial Billing – Customer Does Not WaitGIVEN: A customer arrives with a valid prescription. Some drugs are unavailable. No acceptable alternatives exist.WHEN: The customer does not want to wait for available stocks.THEN: The front desk initiates billing for only the available drugs. Records the missing drug. A notification task is auto-created for inventory admin to expedite reorder.TO DO: Implement notification task creation and assignment to Inventory Admin.UC-BIL-004: Partial Billing – Customer Agrees to WaitGIVEN: A customer arrives with a valid prescription. Some drugs are unavailable. No acceptable alternatives exist.WHEN: The customer agrees to wait for stocks.THEN: Initiates billing for available drugs. Sends a reminder to both Billing Admin and Inventory Admin. Logs a customer waiting record. Sends SMS/app notification to the customer when the drug becomes available.UC-BIL-005: No Drugs AvailableGIVEN: A customer arrives with a valid prescription.WHEN: The front desk validates the prescription. No drugs are available.THEN: The front desk records all missing drugs in the shortage diary. A notification task is raised for Inventory Admin. Customer is informed with an estimated restocking date if known.UC-BIL-006: Monthly Subscription Billing – All Drugs AvailableGIVEN: A customer with a valid monthly subscription arrives with a valid prescription.WHEN: All prescribed drugs are available.THEN: Front desk adds items to the current subscription cycle and initiates approval request to the Billing Admin.BUSINESS RULE: Subscription items must not be billed for immediate payment; they are batched and approved monthly.UC-BIL-007: Monthly Subscription Billing – Partial Drugs AvailableGIVEN: A monthly subscriber arrives. Some drugs are unavailable.WHEN: Front desk adds available items to subscription. Unavailable items are noted.THEN: Initiates approval for Billing Admin. Sends reminder to Billing Admin and Inventory Admin for missing items.UC-BIL-008: Schedule H/H1/X Drug DispensingGIVEN: A customer presents a prescription containing Schedule H, H1, or X drugs.WHEN: The front desk identifies that the prescription includes controlled substances.THEN: System mandates capture of: prescribing doctor name, doctor registration number, patient name, patient age, and prescription date. Dispensing record is stored with a 3-year retention flag. A separate register entry is created for Schedule X drugs.5.2 Inventory ManagementThe inventory module covers all aspects of receiving, validating, storing, and tracking pharmaceutical stock.UC-INV-001: Full Consignment FulfilmentGIVEN: A shipment arrives at the pharmacy with valid invoice fulfilment bills.WHEN: The Inventory Admin validates the purchase order / invoice against the received bill and finds the invoice was FULLY fulfilled.THEN: Initiates full settlement process: updates stock levels for all items, updates vendor account, processes payment (periodic or instant), closes the PO/invoice.UC-INV-002: Partial Consignment FulfilmentGIVEN: A shipment arrives with valid invoice bills.WHEN: Inventory Admin validates PO against received bill and finds the invoice was PARTIALLY fulfilled.THEN: Initiates partial settlement: updates stock for received items, updates vendor account for delivered quantity, generates a child PO for outstanding items, processes partial payment, keeps the parent PO open.NOTE: A child PO must reference the parent PO for traceability.UC-INV-003: Consignment RejectionGIVEN: A shipment arrives but fails validation (damaged goods, wrong products, expired, quantity mismatch).WHEN: Inventory Admin inspects and rejects the consignment.THEN: Consignment is rejected and logged with rejection reason. A return shipment request is raised to the vendor. No stock update is made. Billing Admin is notified. Vendor account is not debited.UC-INV-004: Expiry TrackingGIVEN: Medicines in stock are approaching their expiry date (configurable threshold, default: 90 days).WHEN: The system identifies near-expiry items during daily batch check.THEN: An expiry management task is auto-created. Expiry Management Admin is notified. Option to return to vendor (if return window open), offer discount to customers, or write off stock.UC-INV-005: New Drug OnboardingGIVEN: A new drug not currently in the system master data is required.WHEN: Inventory Admin initiates a new drug addition request.THEN: Request is raised with: drug name, composition, Schedule classification, HSN code, manufacturer, MRP. Super Admin or Invoicing Admin reviews and approves. Drug is added to the master catalogue.5.3 Purchase Order ManagementThe PO module manages the full lifecycle of purchase orders from creation through dispatch, vendor bill validation, and closure.UC-PO-001: New PO Creation (New Vendors)GIVEN: Inventory Admin or Billing Admin initiates a new purchase order.WHEN: Admin selects the invoice / items required, then selects vendors (new vendor path).THEN: System generates POs for selected vendors. POs are dispatched. Billing Admin is notified. PO status is set to &apos;Dispatched&apos;.NOTE: Dispatch PO – TBD on manual vs automated vendor selection process.UC-PO-002: Child PO Creation (Partial Fulfilment)GIVEN: A parent PO was only partially fulfilled.WHEN: Inventory Admin creates a child PO for outstanding items, selecting parent PO reference.THEN: System generates child PO linked to parent. Child PO is dispatched to same or different vendor. Billing Admin is notified of child PO dispatch.UC-PO-003: PO Approval WorkflowGIVEN: A new PO is created by Inventory or Billing Admin.WHEN: PO moves to Invoicing Admin for final approval.THEN: Invoicing Admin reviews and approves / rejects. On approval: PO status updates to &apos;Approved&apos;, vendors are notified. On rejection: requester is notified with reason. Invoicing Admin may modify before approving.UC-PO-004: Vendor Bill Verification & PO ClosureGIVEN: Vendor delivers goods. Inventory Admin verifies delivery against PO.WHEN: PO Verification step: Inventory Admin cross-checks delivery note with PO.THEN: If verified: PO status updated. Vendor Payment Processing initiated. Billing Admin notified. PO marked as fulfilled and closed. If rejected: Reject PO Process triggered. Vendor notified of discrepancy.5.4 Quotation ManagementThe quotation module allows the pharmacy to solicit price quotes from multiple vendors before raising a Purchase Order.UC-QUO-001: Quote Creation ProcessGIVEN: Inventory Admin needs to procure items and wants competitive pricing.WHEN: Admin initiates Quote Creation. Selects invoice/items. Selects multiple vendors.THEN: System sends quotation requests to all selected vendors simultaneously. Vendors respond with pricing. Admin reviews all quotations.PARALLEL PROCESS: On acceptance of best quote – PO Creation Process is triggered AND all other quotations are closed simultaneously.UC-QUO-002: Quotation FulfilmentGIVEN: A vendor responds to a sent quotation.WHEN: System receives vendor response and updates quotation record.THEN: Quotation fulfilment outcome is either: Fully Fulfilled (vendor can supply all items at quoted price) or Not Fulfilled (vendor cannot supply or no response).NOTE: Reconsider path exists — if no acceptable quote received, the process loops back to send updated quotation requests.5.5 Discount & GST ManagementIndian pharmacies apply item-level discounts and GST at different slabs per medicine. This module handles the configuration and application of both.UC-DIS-001: Per-Medicine Discount ConfigurationGIVEN: Inventory Admin or Invoicing Admin wants to configure discounts for specific medicines.WHEN: Admin navigates to medicine master and sets the discount percentage per item.THEN: Discounts are stored at medicine-item level. The billing system applies the configured discount automatically during invoice generation. Discounts can be time-bound (promotional). Discount approval required for above-threshold discounts.UC-DIS-002: GST Slab AssignmentEach medicine is assigned a GST slab at the time of master data creation. The following slabs apply in India:GST SlabApplicable ToHSN Range0% (Nil)Essential life-saving drugs notified by government3001-3006 (select items)5%Medicines under schedule formulations3003, 3004 (bulk)12%Medical devices, diagnostic kits9018, 9021 (select)18%Nutraceuticals, OTC cosmetics, sanitisers3307, 3808 (select)GST on every customer invoice must show CGST + SGST split for intra-state transactions and IGST for inter-state. A GST-compliant invoice must include: pharmacy GSTIN, customer GSTIN (if B2B), HSN code per line, taxable value, and tax amount per slab.5.6 Vendor Management & RankingPharmaSys supports a vendor ecosystem. Vendor ranking features are inspired by the FarmaRack platform as referenced by stakeholders.UC-VEN-001: Vendor OnboardingVendor name, contact details, Drug License number, GSTIN, bank detailsProduct portfolio mapping (which medicines the vendor supplies)Credit terms (payment days, credit limit)Ranking / rating initialisationUC-VEN-002: Vendor RankingRanking criteria: delivery reliability, pricing competitiveness, return acceptance, expiry replacementAutomated ranking based on fulfilment rate, average delivery days, rejection rateManual rating adjustment by Billing or Inventory AdminFarmaRack integration stub: vendor data can be imported from FarmaRack API (future)UC-VEN-003: Vendor Payment & Debt ManagementAccounting Admin tallies PO with received consignment before authorising paymentPeriodic payment plan: amounts added to ledger for batch payment at agreed intervalsInstant payment: processed immediately on PO closureOutstanding balance tracking and reminders to vendors with overdue accounts5.7 Pharmacy Diary & Daily NotesThe digital diary module captures day-to-day operational notes that pharmacists currently maintain manually.Diary Entry TypeDescriptionWho Can AddMedicine Shortage NoteRecord shortages observed during the dayFront Desk, Billing AdminExpiry NoteNear-expiry items flagged by staff manuallyInventory AdminDaily Selling NoteFast-moving vs slow-moving drugs of the dayBilling AdminMedicine Order NoteItems to be included in next POInventory Admin, Billing AdminBakhi (Dues) NoteCustomer credit / outstanding duesBilling Admin, Accounting Admin6.
